GENERAL CONTRACT FOR CONSTRUCTION SERVICES
Mission Bay Hospital — San Francisco, CA
Contract Value: $400,000,000
Contract Version: v1.0

================================================================================
SECTION 3 — SCOPE OF WORK
================================================================================

3.1 BASE SCOPE
The General Contractor shall furnish all labor, materials, equipment, and services
necessary to complete the construction of Mission Bay Hospital as described in the
Contract Documents. The base scope includes all work identified in the drawings
and specifications issued as of the contract execution date.

3.2 ELECTRICAL WORK
All electrical work within the base scope includes standard conduit runs, panel
installations, and lighting as identified in the MEP drawings (Revision 3, dated
January 15, 2024). Any electrical work required for specialized medical equipment
rooms — including MRI suites, CT scan rooms, and surgical theaters — is explicitly
EXCLUDED from the base scope and shall be treated as a change order requiring
owner approval regardless of dollar amount, due to the specialized shielding and
power requirements involved.

3.3 STRUCTURAL MODIFICATIONS
Structural modifications not shown on the approved structural drawings (Revision 2,
dated January 10, 2024) are EXCLUDED from the base scope. Any request to alter
load-bearing elements, add openings in shear walls, or modify the foundation system
shall be treated as out-of-scope work and routed directly to the owner's structural
engineer for review before any approval is granted.

3.4 MECHANICAL AND PLUMBING
Standard HVAC installation, plumbing rough-in, and medical gas piping as specified
in the MEP package are INCLUDED in the base scope. Modifications to the medical gas
system beyond the approved layout, or additions of HVAC zones not shown on the
approved drawings, are EXCLUDED and require a change order.

3.5 CIVIL AND SITE WORK
All site grading, paving, underground utilities, and landscaping shown on the civil
drawings (Revision 1, dated December 20, 2023) are INCLUDED. Additional site work
beyond the approved civil package requires a change order and civil engineer review.

================================================================================
SECTION 7 — CHANGE ORDER PROCEDURES
================================================================================

7.1 CHANGE ORDER THRESHOLD AND APPROVAL AUTHORITY
Change orders shall be approved according to the following dollar thresholds:
  - Up to $25,000:     Site Superintendent approval required
  - $25,001–$100,000:  Project Manager approval required
  - $100,001–$500,000: Owner's Representative approval required
  - Above $500,000:    Owner (Mission Bay Health System) approval required

All out-of-scope change orders, regardless of dollar value, require Owner approval.

7.2 CHANGE ORDER DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
All change orders must include: scope description, cost breakdown, schedule impact
assessment, reference to the contract section being modified, and subcontractor
certification that the work is not included in the base contract price.

7.3 AUDIT AND RECORD RETENTION
All approved change orders and supporting documentation must be retained for a
minimum of seven (7) years following project completion. The General Contractor
is responsible for maintaining a complete and auditable change order log.

================================================================================
SECTION 12 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION
================================================================================

12.1 CHANGE ORDER DISPUTES
Any dispute regarding scope inclusion or exclusion shall first be submitted in
writing to the Owner's Representative within 14 days of the disputed work being
performed. Failure to submit within 14 days constitutes acceptance of the
original contract price for that work.

12.2 ARBITRATION
Unresolved disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration under the American
Arbitration Association Construction Industry Rules. The prevailing party shall
be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and arbitration costs.
